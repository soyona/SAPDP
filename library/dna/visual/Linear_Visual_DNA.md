## DNA Identity

DNA Statement:
Linear Visual DNA expresses high-speed product execution through dark precision, low-friction density, sharp hierarchy, keyboard-native focus, restrained contrast, and operational calm.

---

## DNA Summary

Summary:
Linear works visually because it makes complex product and engineering work feel fast, structured, focused, and under control. Its sensory expression avoids decorative clutter and uses dark surfaces, precise spacing, compact density, crisp typography, subtle motion, and high-signal interface hierarchy.

This Visual DNA excludes task workflow rules, issue state mechanics, roadmap logic, collaboration process, retention systems, implementation details, design tokens, exact color values, keyboard shortcuts, and component definitions. Those belong to Product DNA, UX Specification, or implementation artifacts.

## Core Visual Characteristics

### Characteristic 1

Name:
Dark Precision Surface

Description:
Dark or subdued surfaces create a focused execution environment with controlled contrast.

Perception Effect:
Users perceive the product as serious, technical, and optimized for sustained work.

### Characteristic 2

Name:
Compact Operational Density

Description:
Information is shown in compact rows, lists, sidebars, panels, and command-like surfaces.

Perception Effect:
Users perceive speed and throughput without unnecessary visual weight.

### Characteristic 3

Name:
Crisp Hierarchy

Description:
Typography, spacing, separators, status indicators, and alignment create clear scanning paths.

Perception Effect:
Users can distinguish priority, state, and structure quickly.

### Characteristic 4

Name:
Low-Noise Interface Chrome

Description:
Controls, borders, and icons are present but visually restrained.

Perception Effect:
The interface feels efficient and professional rather than decorative.

### Characteristic 5

Name:
Keyboard-Native Perception

Description:
The visual system implies command speed, shortcuts, and flow even when not explicitly showing interaction logic.

Perception Effect:
Users perceive the product as optimized for expert execution.

### Characteristic 6

Name:
Subtle Motion Feedback

Description:
Motion is quick, restrained, and functional.

Perception Effect:
The product feels responsive and refined without distracting from work.

### Characteristic 7

Name:
Technical Calm

Description:
The visual system avoids heavy decoration while preserving enough polish to feel modern and premium.

Perception Effect:
Users perceive the product as capable, focused, and reliable.

### Characteristic 8

Name:

Small visual signals communicate state, ownership, priority, and progress without heavy ornamentation.

Perception Effect:

## Visual Interaction Model

Interaction Description:
Linear visual experience begins with a focused operational surface. The user sees structured lists, compact navigation, clear status cues, and restrained controls. Density is high but organized. Motion and contrast are tuned for speed rather than drama. The product visually communicates that work can move quickly without losing control.

## Visual Principles

### Principle 1
Visual density must support speed, not clutter.

### Principle 2
Hierarchy should make operational state scannable.

### Principle 3
Decorative elements should be minimized unless they improve clarity.

### Principle 4
Dark surfaces should create focus, not obscure information.

### Principle 5
Motion should feel quick, precise, and functional.

### Principle 6
The interface should visually support expert workflows without excluding basic comprehension.

### Principle 7
Status and priority signals should be subtle but immediately readable.

### Principle 8
The product should feel calm even when representing complex work.

## Risks

### Known Risks

* Dense interfaces can intimidate novice users.
* Dark precision can feel cold if the product needs warmth.
* Subtle status cues can be missed if contrast is too low.
* Expert-oriented visual rhythm can weaken onboarding.
* Over-minimal chrome can reduce discoverability.
* Speed-focused visual language can feel inappropriate for reflective or emotional products.
