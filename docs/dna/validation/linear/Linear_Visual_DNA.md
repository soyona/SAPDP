## DNA Identity

DNA Statement:
Calm, compact work surfaces use restrained contrast, consistent controls, and precise hierarchy to make dense information feel fast, legible, and focused.

---

## DNA Summary

Summary:
Linear's visual language combines information-dense layouts with low-noise surfaces. Dim peripheral navigation, brighter primary content, compact neutral typography, small geometric icons, thin separators, and selective accent color establish hierarchy without decoration. Motion is brief and functional, clarifying selection, disclosure, navigation, and changes of state while preserving a stable workspace.

* Dense information remains readable through alignment, tonal layering, consistent controls, and selective emphasis rather than through large spacing or ornamental separation.

## Core Visual Characteristics

### Characteristic 1

Name:
Tonal Focus Hierarchy

Description:
Primary content uses the clearest text and surface contrast while navigation, metadata, dividers, and inactive controls use progressively quieter tones. Sidebars are intentionally dimmer than the main content area.

Perception Effect:
The workspace feels calm and immediately scannable even when many controls and records are visible.

### Characteristic 2

Name:
Compact Structural Density

Description:
Lists, panels, menus, headers, and property groups place related information in compact rows and bounded regions. Alignment, repetition, thin separators, and subtle surface changes provide structure without generous whitespace.

Perception Effect:
The interface feels fast, capable, and information-rich without appearing chaotic.

### Characteristic N

Name:
Restrained Precision

Description:
Neutral sans-serif typography, concise labels, small geometric icons, modest corner rounding, low-contrast borders, and sparse accent color create a controlled visual vocabulary. Illustration is subordinate to product UI and typically appears as abstract atmosphere or polished interface imagery.

Perception Effect:
The product feels deliberate, technical, and premium rather than decorative or playful.

---

## Visual Interaction Model

Interaction Description:
Visual state changes are communicated through localized contrast shifts, selection fills, disclosure, focus treatments, overlays, and short transitions. Menus and panels appear close to their trigger, selected rows become slightly more prominent, and navigation preserves the surrounding spatial frame. Motion is brief, direct, and subordinate to the information being changed.

## Dominant Visual Signals

### Dominant Visual Signals

* Dark or neutral layered surfaces; bright primary text; muted secondary text; dim peripheral navigation; compact rows; persistent alignment; thin dividers; consistent headers and view controls.

### Secondary Visual Signals

* Small geometric icons; restrained accent colors; softly rounded menus and panels; subtle selection fills; minimal abstract glow or gradient used outside core work surfaces.

---

## Visual Principles

### Principle 1

Use contrast selectively: make the current content plane and active state clear while allowing surrounding navigation, metadata, and inactive controls to recede.

### Principle 2

Support density with consistent alignment, repeated row geometry, concise typography, uniform icons, and subtle separators before adding whitespace or containers.

### Principle N

Keep visual feedback immediate and local. Motion, overlays, and state emphasis should clarify the changed relationship without destabilizing the workspace or competing with content.

## Applicable Scenarios

## Non-Applicable Scenarios

* Expressive entertainment interfaces, child-directed products, illustration-led storytelling, celebratory consumer experiences, or sparse editorial layouts where visual emotion should dominate operational clarity.

## Combination Guidance

### Compatible DNA Characteristics

* Visual systems that share neutral typography, disciplined grids, compact information display, quiet surfaces, restrained color, precise iconography, and functional motion.

### Potential Conflict Characteristics

* Highly ornamental surfaces, broad palettes with many simultaneous accents, oversized display typography inside work views, loose asymmetrical composition, persistent decorative animation, or illustration that competes with operational content.

## Risks

### Known Risks

* Excessive tonal restraint can reduce discoverability or contrast. Compact density can become cramped when alignment and hierarchy are inconsistent. Repeated dark surfaces can flatten hierarchy if their tonal steps are too subtle. Copying the aesthetic without its disciplined consistency can produce generic dark-mode styling rather than the intended calm precision.

## Optional Research Notes

### Historical Notes

Linear's March 2026 refresh explicitly refined its established visual system toward a calmer and more consistent interface.

### Evolution Notes

The refresh strengthened cross-view consistency, redrew and resized icons, and further separated peripheral navigation from primary content through tonal emphasis.

### Alternative Interpretations

Linear can appear minimalist at first glance, but its defining quality is controlled density rather than sparse composition.

### Research Notes

The reusable pattern is not dark mode alone. It is the coordination of tonal hierarchy, compact geometry, typography, icon consistency, and restrained motion across dense work surfaces.
