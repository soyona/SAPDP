# Animal Crossing Official Product Evidence Register

Retrieved: 2026-06-19

DNA Type: `Product DNA`

Product scope: Animal Crossing, represented by Nintendo's official *Animal Crossing: New Horizons* product reality.

Governance stage: `Reality Validation`

This register contains only factual product-behavior observations published by Nintendo. It does not materialize a Product DNA asset or infer reusable product rules. No blogs, reviews, forums, wikis, AI-generated content, personal opinions, or unsupported statements are included.

## PROD-EVID-001 — Open-ended island life tied to real time

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/explore/
- Observed evidence: Nintendo describes an open-ended island routine in which the player can garden, fish, decorate, hunt for bugs and fossils, and interact with animal residents at their own pace. Time of day and seasons match real life, and island activity continues whether or not the player is present.

## PROD-EVID-002 — Time- and season-dependent collection

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/explore/
- Observed evidence: Insects, sea creatures, and fish vary by season and time of day. A newly discovered creature is automatically added to the in-game Critterpedia. New bugs, fish, and analyzed fossils can be donated to the museum.

## PROD-EVID-003 — Daily goals and Nook Miles rewards

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/explore/
- Observed evidence: The in-game NookPhone tracks daily goals, collected insects and fish, learned DIY recipes, and earned Nook Miles. Nintendo states that players earn Nook Miles through special activities or ordinary daily island routines and can spend Miles on new recipes, larger pocket capacity, and other rewards.

## PROD-EVID-004 — Resource-to-crafting loop

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/create/
- Observed evidence: Nintendo presents crafting as a three-step sequence: learn DIY recipes through island activities, gather natural materials such as branches, stones, and weeds, then use the required materials at Tom Nook's workbench. Crafted tools enable acquisition of additional materials, which in turn enable more crafting.

## PROD-EVID-005 — Player-directed customization and expansion

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/create/
- Observed evidence: Players choose where to place their tent, can later upgrade to a house, and may place and fine-tune furniture inside or outside. As play progresses, home storage becomes available. Players can also contribute resources and Bells toward shops and homes and build bridges and slopes to improve island navigation.

## PROD-EVID-006 — Recurring discovery through travel

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/create/
- Observed evidence: Dodo Airlines Mystery Island Tours take the player to nearby deserted islands where they can gather resources, catch bugs and fish, and find fruit or flowers that may not be native to their own island.

## PROD-EVID-007 — Resident-centered community growth

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/share/
- Observed evidence: Nintendo describes animal residents as a source of daily surprises. The player chooses a location for a resident's tent, can talk with residents at any time, and grows the island community at their own pace. Compatible Animal Crossing amiibo figures or cards can invite specific characters to the campsite or the Roost café.

## PROD-EVID-008 — Shared play and island visits

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/share/
- Official product page: https://www.nintendo.com/us/store/products/animal-crossing-new-horizons-switch/
- Observed evidence: Up to eight account holders can live on one island; with one Nintendo Switch system and one game, up to four can play on that island simultaneously. With additional systems and games, up to eight players can play together through local wireless or online play. Visitors can gather resources, obtain fruit, shop, view museum collections, and meet at the Roost.

## PROD-EVID-009 — Non-destructive discovery and inspiration

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/share/
- Observed evidence: Dream visits let players explore islands by island name, Dream Address, or random selection. Actions taken during a dream are not saved on the visited island. Nintendo also documents an in-game Happy Home Network Showroom where players can view published vacation-home examples, follow designers, and use those examples for inspiration.

## PROD-EVID-010 — Client-request design loop in official DLC

- Official source: https://animalcrossing.nintendo.com/new-horizons/happy-home-paradise/
- Observed evidence: In the official Happy Home Paradise expansion, clients arrive with vacation-home requests. The player learns the client's request, chooses and customizes exterior and interior elements, the yard, and weather, and can furnish from a built-in inventory that is not limited by the player's own inventory, recipes, or resources. Finished designs can be photographed and shared through the Happy Home Network.
