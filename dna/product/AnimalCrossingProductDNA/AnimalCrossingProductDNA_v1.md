# DNA Type

`Product DNA`

# Experience Pattern

- `EXP-01`: Center the experience on a player-paced place that supports multiple parallel activities, including collecting, crafting, customization, resident interaction, and shared visits, without imposing one required activity order. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-001`, `PROD-EVID-004`, `PROD-EVID-007`, `PROD-EVID-008`.
- `EXP-02`: Tie the place to real-world time and seasons so available discoveries and current activity can vary when the player returns. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-001`, `PROD-EVID-002`.
- `EXP-03`: Let player actions accumulate into a personalized home and community through object placement, home growth, public contributions, and navigational infrastructure. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-005`.
- `EXP-04`: Support both solitary creation and social discovery by letting players host, visit, gather, shop, inspect collections, and obtain inspiration without replacing the owner's persistent place. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-008`, `PROD-EVID-009`.

# Interaction Pattern

- `INT-01`: When a player discovers a new creature, record it automatically in a persistent collection and offer a separate contribution path to the museum. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-002`.
- `INT-02`: Implement crafting as an explicit sequence of learning a recipe, gathering its materials, and using a workbench; let crafted tools unlock acquisition of additional materials for subsequent crafting. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-004`.
- `INT-03`: Give the player direct choice over where homes, furniture, bridges, and slopes are placed, and allow furniture arrangements to be fine-tuned after placement. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-005`.
- `INT-04`: Provide travel actions that move the player to other islands for resources, discoveries, social play, or inspiration, while distinguishing persistent multiplayer visits from non-destructive dream visits. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-006`, `PROD-EVID-008`, `PROD-EVID-009`.
- `INT-05`: For request-driven design activities, first expose a client's requested outcome, then provide the relevant furnishing choices, and finish with a result that can be photographed and shared. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-010`.

# Motivation Pattern

- `MOT-01`: Convert both special activities and ordinary daily routines into a visible reward currency that can be exchanged for recipes, capacity increases, and other functional rewards. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-003`.
- `MOT-02`: Make discovery completion legible through automatic collection records and optional museum contributions. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-002`.
- `MOT-03`: Preserve player authorship by allowing choices over home location, object arrangement, and community infrastructure. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-005`.
- `MOT-04`: Use resident arrivals, resident conversations, shared visits, and published player creations as sources of social novelty and inspiration. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-007`, `PROD-EVID-008`, `PROD-EVID-009`.
- `MOT-05`: In request-driven design, pair a defined client need with broad furnishing freedom so completion satisfies an external request while preserving creative choice. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-010`.

# Retention Pattern

- `RET-01`: Change discoverable creatures and island conditions by real-world time and season so returning at different times can reveal different content. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-001`, `PROD-EVID-002`.
- `RET-02`: Maintain a daily-goal surface that tracks routine activity, learned recipes, collection state, and earned rewards. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-003`.
- `RET-03`: Provide repeatable trips to nearby islands where resource, creature, fruit, or flower availability can differ from the player's home island. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-006`.
- `RET-04`: Sustain an evolving community through resident interaction and allow return visits with other players for gathering, shopping, collection viewing, and shared activities. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-007`, `PROD-EVID-008`.
- `RET-05`: Provide non-destructive visits to published places and designs so players can repeatedly explore others' work for inspiration without modifying the source. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-009`.

# Progression Pattern

- `PROG-01`: Begin with a freely placed tent, permit advancement to a house, and unlock built-in home storage as play progresses. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-005`.
- `PROG-02`: Award Nook Miles for special activities and daily routines, then let players spend those Miles on new recipes and larger carrying capacity. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-003`.
- `PROG-03`: Make crafting self-extending: learned recipes and gathered materials produce tools, and those tools provide access to materials for additional items. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-004`.
- `PROG-04`: Let players advance the shared island by contributing resources and Bells toward shops and homes and by adding bridges and slopes that improve navigation. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-005`.
- `PROG-05`: Grow collection progress by recording new creatures in the Critterpedia and accepting new species and analyzed fossils at the museum. Evidence: `PROD-EVID-002`.
